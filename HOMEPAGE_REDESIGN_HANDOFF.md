# Homepage Redesign Handoff

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Scope and guardrail

This document carries the homepage concept exploration across sessions. The public `/` homepage remains unchanged until a direction is approved. Exploratory concepts belong here and in the private concept lab, not in `DESIGN_DECISIONS.md`.

## Current objections

- The opening is technically careful but aesthetically unresolved.
- The dark forest field still reads as an oversized container that the thesis and actions have been placed inside.
- The portrait is dignified and truthful, but the forest / portrait / purpose triptych divides one idea into three competing compartments.
- The purpose field is so quiet and narrow that it reads underfilled rather than deliberate.
- The evidence rail is accurate but makes the first transition procedural before the emotional entrance has landed.
- The stepped thesis, narrower forest field, and bottom-anchored actions are successive corrections to a weak premise, not a durable composition.
- The later page—especially Story, Model, and the Human Bookend—outperforms the opening.

## Truth that must remain intact

- Rebuild & Rise is a Nigerian organization founded by two Nigerian university students and is registered in Nigeria.
- The 2022 Abuja archive records the founders' prior relief work. It is not Pilot 001, current Rebuild & Rise activity, or impact evidence.
- Pilot 001 is in design.
- Future health, education, skills, and community-capacity language describes intended areas, not active services or proven outcomes.
- Communities are partners, not projects. No drop-and-go claims, invented partners, metrics, testimonials, outcomes, or beneficiary identities.
- Archive, live, representative, texture, and illustration treatments must remain distinguishable.
- Stock or third-party documentary photography can be representative context only unless it truthfully documents Rebuild & Rise activity.
- Images containing children or vulnerable people require especially strict consent, dignity, and context review.

## Successful ideas worth preserving

- “We build what stays.”
- Moderate display scale and reliable text reflow.
- Fraunces / Manrope / mono hierarchy and the warm forest, cream, parchment, ivory, walnut, and olive palette.
- Static first paint, no fixed hero height, no text over faces, natural image ratios, external captions, and explicit provenance.
- Who We Serve as a relational ledger rather than beneficiary cards.
- Story's preparation / working-scene / material-evidence sequence.
- The lessons-to-Model transition and the seven-step Model as the sole high-detail signature moment.
- The one-time interlude after the Model.
- Program areas as an open working directory rather than illustrated cards.
- Pilot 001's truthful field ledger and “In design” status.
- Full-color, unfiltered founder headshots.
- The Human Bookend finale with a real group photograph and practical participation routes.
- D47's corrected Contact destination and all working navigation/form behavior.

## Failed ideas that must not return

- The forest / portrait / purpose hero premise as another minor variation.
- Oversized dark-green panels that need decorative objects to justify their space.
- Tree, root, seed, doorway, arch, threshold, hanging-cord, or terminal-door geometry.
- Document-spanning decorative Path outside the Model.
- Equal feature cards, repeated rounded containers, startup icon grids, or four-way participation matrices.
- Retired audience and program engravings or illustrated panels.
- Full-bleed wallpaper with scrims or text across people.
- Decorative archive labels attached to everything.
- AI-generated people, generated humanitarian scenes, heavy duotones, pity imagery, or representative photography presented as field evidence.
- The camp landscape as aesthetic atmosphere; it risks turning hardship into scenery.
- The Uganda workshop photograph on the homepage; it weakens Nigerian specificity.

## Proposed directions and current rank

1. **A Record in Three Movements** — chronological archive sequence: preparation, gathering, material evidence, then the lesson. Current recommendation after live review.
2. **The Abuja Chapter** — light documentary cover; thesis/purpose first, followed by one broad human record. Runner-up.
3. **The Quiet Declaration** — light typographic opening whose lower edge reveals the human record; information fills the restraint.
4. **Under the Canopy** — one exceptional wide Nigerian gathering as the entrance, with a shallow separate declaration band. Requires new photography before advancing.
5. **Hands Before Claims** — process-led opening centered on active preparation and work rather than a beneficiary portrait.
6. **The Civic Ledger** — accountable public working record; credible but currently ranked last because it risks bureaucracy and repeats the site's over-restraint.

The first four are selected for the concept lab. Full 19-point specifications and final ranking are in `HOMEPAGE_CREATIVE_DIRECTIONS.md`. The Civic Ledger remains a useful rejection/control case but will not be prototyped unless needed.

## Review findings by breakpoint

### Current baseline

- **1440:** The portrait is the only magnet. Forest and purpose read as flanking compartments; the evidence rail converts emotion into administration too quickly. Later Story, Model, and finale are more authored.
- **1280:** This is the awkward edge of D47. The portrait dominates a rigid central monument; the forest remains visibly underfilled and the narrow purpose column looks cautious.
- **1024:** The composition stops being a triptych, but the source order becomes thesis slab → large portrait → purpose/actions later. The first viewport has no mission, action, or status, so the opening feels less coherent rather than more responsive.
- **768:** The same stack is longer and more modular. The complete portrait is dignified, but the opening behaves like separate blocks rather than one chapter.
- **375:** Human presence arrives immediately after the thesis, but the portrait consumes most of the first viewport and purpose/action fall below it. The opening is human yet narratively incomplete.
- **320:** The two-line thesis and complete portrait remain intact with zero overflow. The photograph begins promptly, but the same missing first-view purpose/action problem remains.

### A Record in Three Movements

- **1440:** Strongest five-second identity and most authored composition. The forest masthead is compact enough to avoid D47's dead panel, while the archive sequence makes a specific argument.
- **1024:** The masthead and two-image lead remain readable; the chronological rail initially caused 26px overflow and was corrected by switching to the tablet system at 1100px and allowing caption wrapping.
- **768:** The first two images preserve a clear dominant/supporting hierarchy; the opening remains recognizably different from desktop rather than collapsing into a generic stack.
- **375:** Thesis, purpose, and actions fit before the first image. The photograph begins in the first viewport and the sequence remains legible.
- **320:** No horizontal overflow; title remains 40px and two lines; the first working image begins at the end of the first viewport. The opening is long but still shorter than the current homepage overall because repeated Story photography was removed.
- **Weakest composition:** The first implementation repeated the opening archive images again in Story. That repetition was removed; the origin chapter now turns directly into the three lessons.

### The Abuja Chapter

- **1440:** Cleanest and lowest-risk alternative. The thesis and mission finally belong to one field, and the broad record reads as the next sentence.
- **1024 / 768:** The declaration stacks cleanly; status remains a ruled source line and the image retains 4:3.
- **375 / 320:** Zero overflow and moderate title scale. At 320 the image begins near the bottom of the first viewport; this is acceptable but less immediate than Three Movements or Quiet Declaration.
- **Weakest composition:** The current `groupTree03` image is posed, child-heavy, and visually busy. It is a truthful placeholder, not a recommended production hero. A commissioned listening/gathering frame would materially improve the concept.

### The Quiet Declaration

- **1440:** Calm and credible, with the portrait and Now register giving the right field enough purpose. Still the most vulnerable to feeling over-restrained.
- **1024 / 768:** The narrower document system is coherent and the portrait remains distinct from the text.
- **375 / 320:** The first implementation put the documentary-standard note before the image, pushing human presence below the first viewport. The image now comes immediately after the actions and begins within the first viewport; status/standard follow it.
- **Weakest composition:** The first desktop version had too much empty upper-right space. Enlarging the right field and placing the portrait directly beneath the Now register corrected the imbalance, but the concept remains deliberately quiet.

### Under the Canopy

- **1440:** Most immediate human presence and strongest photographic scale.
- **1024 / 768:** The original image-first order pushed the thesis below the first viewport. The declaration band now precedes the image at 1100px and below.
- **375 / 320:** Thesis, status, and actions lead cleanly into a 4:3 record with no wallpaper crop or overflow.
- **Weakest composition:** The available wide archive image is a posed, child-heavy relief group. The composition is not ethically or visually strong enough for a production hero. Keep the system as a prototype, but reject advancement until a better consented Nigerian gathering/listening image is approved.

### Shared full-page findings

- Every prototype preserves the Model as the sole connected, high-detail visual climax.
- Story/process, programs, Pilot status, and finale all use canonical copy. The four systems diverge through chronology, width, color distribution, photo hierarchy, and section rhythm rather than palette swaps.
- All concepts were reviewed at 1440, 1024, 768, 375, and 320 with zero horizontal overflow.
- Mobile page lengths remain substantial because the canonical story is substantial, but all prototypes are shorter than the current baseline: at 375 the current baseline measured about 12,369px, versus about 9,959px for Three Movements, 10,100px for Abuja Chapter, 10,059px for Under the Canopy, and 10,349px for Quiet Declaration.
- Prototype navigation now includes a native, keyboard-accessible mobile menu and preserves the separate partnership action.

## Current recommendation

**A Record in Three Movements** is the current leader. The live comparison showed that its chronological archive sequence is more specific, memorable, and authored than the other systems. It replaces D47's three adjacent compartments with an actual narrative: preparation → gathering → what remained. The origin chapter then converts that record into lessons, and the Model becomes the earned answer.

Runner-up: **The Abuja Chapter**. It is calmer, lighter, easier to sustain, and has the lowest image-production burden once one strong landscape record is secured.

## Approved implementation — 2026-07-14

The recommended direction, **A Record in Three Movements**, was approved and is
now implemented on the public homepage. D48 records the accepted production
system in `DESIGN_DECISIONS.md`; the earlier concept directions remain in this
handoff and the concept lab for historical comparison.

### Final production decisions

- The public Header remains unchanged. The opening begins with a compact forest
  declaration containing the thesis, canonical purpose statement, and two
  existing actions.
- The approved archive sequence uses `packingTeam`, `communityWomen`, and
  `mealsPrepared` with their canonical alts, captions, stamps, and confirmed
  archive status.
- Movement labels are `Prepared`, `Gathered`, and `Meals prepared`. The lab's
  `What remained?` label was not promoted because it could overstate what the
  final photograph documents.
- A plural canonical provenance note says the photographs record the founders'
  prior Abuja relief work in 2022 and do not depict Pilot 001 or current impact.
- The Story photograph spread is removed to prevent immediate repetition. The
  canonical origin copy now leads directly to the three lessons.
- The exact public Model, JourneyPath, later editorial ledgers, founders,
  correspondence behavior, Human Bookend, footer, routes, metadata, and
  secondary pages are preserved.
- No external candidate was downloaded, licensed, or published. The 30-image
  research pool remains an acquisition resource for a future commissioned shoot.

### Final review findings

- **1440:** the 63.36px thesis and purpose share a compact declaration; the
  three frames read immediately as dominant record, human counterpoint, and
  material detail. The status closes the sequence without becoming a badge.
- **1280:** the system retains the complete wide editorial spread with no
  caption collision or status overflow.
- **1024:** the first pass stacked the declaration too early and recreated an
  unused forest field. The final system keeps a two-column declaration here;
  the masthead is about 268px high and the archive begins around 420px.
- **768:** the declaration becomes a deliberate single-column reading order;
  the archive retains a 4:2 dominant/supporting grid rather than equal tiles.
- **720 enlarged-text proxy:** the document and layout widths match, the thesis
  is 44px, the first image begins around 536px, and captions reflow without
  clipping.
- **375:** the thesis is 44px and the first image begins around 547px. The
  portrait moves right and the material frame returns left. No overflow.
- **320:** the thesis is 40px and the first image begins around 639px, inside an
  812px review viewport. No overflow.
- Story contains no repeated archive images and still exposes both `#story` and
  `[data-path-cards]`. The JourneyPath enters and the exact Model clears its text
  at desktop.
- Mobile menu open/close, focus containment, Escape handling, focus return, and
  body-scroll release pass.
- Semantic review records one H1, no duplicate IDs, no missing image alt
  attributes, no heading-level skips, and an ordered three-item movement list.
  Hero contrast samples range from 5.44:1 to 11.89:1.
- A cold local production pass at 375px recorded about 368 KiB across 23
  resources, with roughly 38 KiB attributed to images. The dominant image alone
  has high fetch priority; the two subordinate frames are lazy. Local navigation
  timing recorded approximately 90ms to DOM content loaded and 176ms to load.
  These local timings are a regression check, not a field performance claim.
- Lint, TypeScript, optimized production build, public route checks, Contact
  redirect, OG image, robots, sitemap, and concept-lab noindex/isolation pass.
