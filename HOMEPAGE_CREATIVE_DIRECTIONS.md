# Homepage Creative Directions

Status: exploration only. None of these directions is approved for the public homepage.

## Ranked order

1. A Record in Three Movements
2. The Abuja Chapter
3. The Quiet Declaration
4. Under the Canopy
5. Hands Before Claims
6. The Civic Ledger

The live prototype review moved **A Record in Three Movements** ahead of the initial written favorite, The Abuja Chapter. The chronological opening is more authored and more specific to Rebuild & Rise. The Abuja Chapter remains the lower-risk runner-up.

## 1. A Record in Three Movements

1. **Working name:** A Record in Three Movements.
2. **Governing idea:** The opening is a short documentary sequence rather than a single hero: preparation, gathering, and material evidence preview the relief-to-systems argument.
3. **Five-second impression:** A human field journal with momentum; the organization feels shaped by observed experience rather than a slogan.
4. **Desktop hero:** A compact forest masthead holds the eyebrow, moderate thesis, purpose, and actions. Beneath it, an asymmetric sequence uses `packingTeam` as a broad working landscape, `communityWomen` as a portrait, and `mealsPrepared` as a small evidence detail. A narrow chronological rail reads Prepared / Gathered / What remained? Status lives at the end of the rail.
5. **Mobile hero:** Thesis, mission, and actions first; the three images then appear as a wide working scene, narrower portrait, and small material detail. Captions remain external. Status follows the sequence.
6. **Image strategy:** Multiple approved archive photographs make a visual argument. No new image is required for the prototype. The later origin chapter removes repeated photography and moves directly to the lessons.
7. **Color distribution:** Compact forest masthead; parchment photographic field; ivory and cream reading chapters; walnut chronology and rules; natural archive color.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis 56–60px desktop and 40–44px mobile; movement labels 11–12px mono; purpose 16–18px; section headings 32–48px.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** Thesis opens the sequence; purpose explains its consequence; status is the honest present tense at the chronological endpoint; action comes before the archive but does not interrupt it.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** The last movement asks what remained after relief; the audience ledger answers by naming the overlapping barriers future systems must address.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Three-frame opening → relational audience ledger → concise origin and lessons → Model climax → interlude → two-column program folios → Pilot ledger → founders → reversed human finale.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Chronology, stepped margins, paired folios, and source captions carry character without archive labels on every object.
13. **Model signature:** The Model remains the one connected diagram and the sole high-detail analytical climax.
14. **Conclusion:** The finale reverses the opening composition: one broad collective image precedes the participation routes and closing thesis.
15. **Preserved:** Canonical copy, approved archive, evidence rules, audience/program/Pilot ledgers, founders, Model logic, Human Bookend.
16. **Removed or changed:** Current triptych hero disappears; Story no longer repeats the three opening images; the immediate administrative evidence rail is absorbed into chronology.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** The opening is denser and mobile is longer. Three early images can exhaust the archive. It requires strict caption discipline to avoid reading as a portfolio contact sheet.
18. **Exact image requirements:** One horizontal working scene, one portrait listening/gathering scene, and one material detail from the same truthful context or clearly separated sources. Existing approved images satisfy the prototype.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** The three frames embody the founding contradiction: the relief mattered, the supplies ended, and the method had to change.

## 2. The Abuja Chapter

1. **Working name:** The Abuja Chapter.
2. **Governing idea:** Begin like a Nigerian documentary publication: one light declaration followed by one broad human record. Thesis and evidence are consecutive, not adjacent compartments.
3. **Five-second impression:** Human, confident, warm, documentary.
4. **Desktop hero:** Ivory title field across a 12-column grid; thesis at 58–62px on the left, mission and actions on the right, status in one ruled line, then a broad 4:3 Abuja archive record with external caption and provenance.
5. **Mobile hero:** Eyebrow → two-line 40–44px thesis → mission → actions → ruled status → complete natural-ratio image → provenance.
6. **Image strategy:** One decisive landscape human photograph opens the page; the women’s portrait returns later or remains in the archive. Prototype uses `groupTree03`.
7. **Color distribution:** Ivory and cream dominate; natural photographic color is second; parchment supports origin; forest becomes action and punctuation rather than architecture.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis 58–62px desktop and 40–44px mobile; purpose 18px; mono status; chapter titles 38–48px.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** Thesis and purpose share one reading field. Status is a source line rather than a badge. Explore the Model is primary; partnership remains quiet.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** The broad record and its caption resolve directly into the relational audience ledger on the same light ground.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Declaration → broad human record → audience ledger → origin photo essay → lessons → Model → interlude → program folios → Pilot ledger → founders → Human Bookend.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Page-edge alignment, open captions, folio numbers, and alternating wide/narrow measures.
13. **Model signature:** Current analytical complexity is preserved and gains contrast from the calmer opening.
14. **Conclusion:** Preserve the collective finale and direct invitation, mirroring the broad opening without copying its geometry.
15. **Preserved:** Canonical copy, archive truth, D30 finale, D33 Pilot ledger, D40 programs, D41 audience ledger, Story process images, Model.
16. **Removed or changed:** Forest thesis slab, portrait overlap, three-column hinge, leaf stop, immediate evidence triptych.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** Depends on a wide human image strong enough to carry the opening. `groupTree03` is truthful but posed and visually busy.
18. **Exact image requirements:** Nigerian 3:2 or 4:3 gathering in Abuja or central/northern Nigeria; people listening or organizing; no distribution; crop-safe sides; natural shade; verified consent/license.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** The design cannot be separated from the Abuja archive → relief limits → seven-step Model sequence.

## 3. The Quiet Declaration

1. **Working name:** The Quiet Declaration.
2. **Governing idea:** Start on ivory with an information-rich typographic declaration; human evidence is the next sentence, not a decorative object.
3. **Five-second impression:** Intelligent, calm, authored, candid about being in formation.
4. **Desktop hero:** Thesis, mission, and actions occupy the left field. A narrow Now register sits on the right, followed by the complete portrait below it. The documentary standard is pushed after the image on mobile.
5. **Mobile hero:** Identifier → 40–44px thesis → mission → actions → complete portrait beginning inside the first viewport → provenance → status and documentary standard.
6. **Image strategy:** One portrait or landscape record; no image overlay. Prototype uses `communityWomen` and later process images.
7. **Color distribution:** Predominantly ivory and parchment; forest is reserved for actions, Model accents, and finale; photography provides the first saturation.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis capped at 64px; hierarchy comes from measure and indentation rather than scale inflation.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** All belong to one document field; status is a marginal annotation and never a separate card.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** Portrait and provenance form a hinge; the audience ledger follows with a stronger top rule.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Declaration → portrait → narrow audience and origin chapters → Model expansion → quiet program/status chapters → populated finale.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Narrower reading widths, exact rules, and image scale changes prevent empty luxury-brand restraint.
13. **Model signature:** The quiet opening gives the Model maximum contrast.
14. **Conclusion:** Human Bookend remains forest-backed and photograph-led so the page ends warmer than it begins.
15. **Preserved:** Typography, captions, ledgers, Model, Pilot language, founders, finale.
16. **Removed or changed:** Forest hero plane, overlap geometry, triptych, decorative punctuation.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** Still vulnerable to feeling too restrained if the portrait begins too low. The corrected prototype moves the portrait before the documentary-standard note on mobile.
18. **Exact image requirements:** Strong natural-ratio archive record with a clear human relationship; negative space is unnecessary because copy never overlays it.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** Authority comes from placing the durable-systems thesis beside the honest “Pilot 001 · In design” present tense.

## 4. Under the Canopy

1. **Working name:** Under the Canopy.
2. **Governing idea:** A real Nigerian gathering provides the opening scale; typography occupies a separate shallow declaration band.
3. **Five-second impression:** Immediate place, collective presence, shade, and seriousness.
4. **Desktop hero:** Wide natural-ratio gathering image immediately beneath navigation; its caption remains external. A shallow forest declaration band follows with thesis, purpose, status, and actions.
5. **Mobile hero:** Declaration band → complete 4:3 image → provenance; no wallpaper crop.
6. **Image strategy:** One exceptional wide Nigerian gathering. Prototype uses `groupTree03`; production should replace it with an approved conversation/listening frame.
7. **Color distribution:** Natural greens, clothing, and earth dominate; forest is a shallow anchor; parchment and walnut support captions.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis 56–60px desktop and 40–44px mobile; shallow band prevents the type from floating in void.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** They form a single caption-like declaration adjacent to the record.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** The broad group resolves into three named audiences without claiming that photographed people belong to those groups.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Immersive entrance → parchment audience ledger → cream origin spread → Model → olive-tinted program chapter → lighter human finale.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Photographic palette seams, marginal captions, and strong alignment.
13. **Model signature:** Immersive visual entrance contrasts with the intellectual Model climax.
14. **Conclusion:** A second distinct gathering closes on parchment rather than another dark metaphor.
15. **Preserved:** Human Arrival, natural image grade, provenance, later ledgers, Model, Human Bookend.
16. **Removed or changed:** Entire current hero geometry and portrait overlap.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** Most image-dependent direction. The approved group archive is posed, child-heavy, and too close to a donor-group photograph for a recommended production hero.
18. **Exact image requirements:** Horizontal 3:2 Nigerian meeting; visible agency and listening; no central donor; no handout; important faces outside crop-risk zones; 3000px+; verified release and location.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** The canopy connects to the actual Abuja archive environment, not a generic pan-African metaphor.

## 5. Hands Before Claims

1. **Working name:** Hands Before Claims.
2. **Governing idea:** Open with preparation and work rather than a beneficiary portrait, shifting attention to method and responsibility.
3. **Five-second impression:** Practical, grounded, unsentimental.
4. **Desktop hero:** Parchment masthead with thesis/purpose/status above a wide packing photograph and smaller meal-evidence inset.
5. **Mobile hero:** Thesis → mission/action → packing image → meal detail → provenance/status.
6. **Image strategy:** Process, hands, tools, notebooks, packing, teaching preparation, and working environments.
7. **Color distribution:** Parchment and walnut lead; working light supplies amber and earth; forest is restrained.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis 56–60px; an origin hinge at 28–32px; mono source lines.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** Thesis is tested against the evidence of past method; purpose names the lesson; status remains compact.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** The page asks what responsible preparation must account for, then names the overlapping barriers.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Process opening → audience → community portrait → lessons → Model → programs → Pilot → founders → collective finale.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Task lines, source captions, and measured notebook margins.
13. **Model signature:** The Model reads as the formalization of the work shown above.
14. **Conclusion:** Travel from hands preparing supplies to a collective invitation.
15. **Preserved:** Packing/meals images, archive ethics, Story logic, Model, Pilot ledger, founders, finale.
16. **Removed or changed:** Community-women hero, triptych, oversized forest mass.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** Can make the organization look like an ongoing food-distribution charity; corrective purpose copy must be immediate. Existing packing photography is visually modest.
18. **Exact image requirements:** Nigerian people actively organizing, making, repairing, teaching, or documenting; real tools; no staged handover, donor props, or passive subjects.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** It begins with the exact relief method the founders outgrew and turns its limits into the Model.

## 6. The Civic Ledger

1. **Working name:** The Civic Ledger.
2. **Governing idea:** Treat the homepage as a public working record: mission, status, archive source, intended communities, method, and open questions.
3. **Five-second impression:** Accountable Nigerian civic institution in formation.
4. **Desktop hero:** Continuous ivory masthead with wordmark, Nigeria, registration, Pilot status, thesis over seven columns, mission over five, then one landscape archive record and horizontal source ledger.
5. **Mobile hero:** Facts → thesis → mission → image → source line → actions.
6. **Image strategy:** Numbered records at meaningful chapter points; representative images remain visibly distinct.
7. **Color distribution:** Ivory dominant, narrow forest folio, walnut rules, leaf only for verified status dots.
8. **Typography hierarchy:** Thesis 56–60px; compact masthead; chapter titles 36–44px; small systematic folios.
9. **Thesis / purpose / status / action:** Public-record fields with thesis first, purpose second, status third, action last.
10. **Transition into Who We Serve:** A contents sequence leads into the first audience chapter.
11. **Full-page pacing:** Masthead → audience register → origin record → Model foldout → program directory → status ledger → team → participation.
12. **Quiet-section character:** Folio numbers, cross-references, source lines, and open questions.
13. **Model signature:** The Model is the one technical foldout inside an otherwise linear publication.
14. **Conclusion:** The formal record relaxes into a human photograph and direct invitation.
15. **Preserved:** Most post-hero editorial systems, especially D33, D40, and D41.
16. **Removed or changed:** Current D47 overlap geometry.
17. **Risks / tradeoffs:** Safest but least emotionally compelling; can feel bureaucratic, academic, or like an annual report.
18. **Exact image requirements:** One landscape archive opener plus two secondary records with strong source documentation.
19. **Why only Rebuild & Rise:** It exposes the organization’s real early stage instead of performing mature impact.

## Recommendation

**Recommend: A Record in Three Movements.** It is the most authored and specific expression of the actual founding logic. It replaces the current triptych with a sequence, makes the archive do narrative work, and creates a visible reason for the Model to exist. Its mobile opening is longer than The Abuja Chapter, but it remains shorter than the current homepage overall because the origin chapter stops repeating the archive.

**Runner-up: The Abuja Chapter.** It is calmer, less demanding, and easier to sustain with one strong image. Choose it if the archive sequence feels too dense or if the first production priority is lower image burden.

**Do not advance without a new photograph:** Under the Canopy. Its governing idea is strong, but the currently approved wide group images are too posed and child-heavy for the role.

**Reject as primary recommendation:** The Civic Ledger. Credibility is already present; emotional entrance and compositional magnetism are the missing qualities.
